Built from a cleaning agency that's operating right now.

Not a course built from research. Not frameworks adapted from someone else's content. A system pulled directly from a real residential cleaning agency currently in operation.

The origin

This playbook exists because of a frustrating gap. When I was building my cleaning agency, there was no shortage of YouTube content, Reddit threads, and generic business courses telling me what to do. But none of them gave me what I actually needed: a word-for-word phone script I could read from on my first call, a job ad I could paste into Indeed that afternoon, or a pricing table with real numbers from real markets.

I had to build all of that myself — through trial, error, and a lot of money left on the table while I figured it out. The playbook is everything I built, packaged so you don't have to start from scratch.

What makes this different from other cleaning business courses

Most cleaning business content is created by people who either cleaned houses themselves or researched the industry from the outside. This playbook is built by someone actively running a cleaning agency as a management and operations business — not doing the cleaning, but hiring, scheduling, pricing, and scaling.

The distinction matters because the assets in this playbook aren't historical. They're live. The phone script is the one the agency uses on incoming calls today. The job ad is the one posted when a new cleaner slot opens. The pricing table reflects what the agency actually charges in current markets. When something stops working, it gets updated — and those updates flow to everyone who has purchased.

  • Module 2 — Hiring Great Cleaners: The word-for-word job ad, phone screening call script, red flag checklist, and test clean protocol.
  • Module 3 — Booking Customers: Full incoming call script, follow-up email template, and real pricing strategy table.
  • Module 4 — Daily Operations: Morning, midday and end-of-day checklists, payment flow, and the money rules that protect you legally.

  • Module 6 — Scaling to $10K+/Month: Phase-by-phase roadmap with specific revenue milestones and the math behind each one.

Who this is for

The playbook is built for people starting a cleaning agency from zero — no industry experience, no employees, no equipment. The entire model is management-based: you hire self-employed cleaners, book clients, coordinate operations, and keep the margin. You never clean a home yourself.

It is not for people already running an established operation looking for advanced growth tactics, or people who want a passive income product that requires no work. Building a cleaning agency takes focused effort in the first 60–90 days. After that, with the right systems in place, it runs largely without you.
